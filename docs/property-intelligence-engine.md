# RoofSignal Property Intelligence Engine

Version: 1.0

## Purpose

This document defines RoofSignal as a staged Property Intelligence company.

The service business is not the destination. It is the vehicle that finances data acquisition, domain expertise, trust, and future software leverage.

The real product is Property Intelligence: structured, actionable knowledge about building condition, maintenance risk, priorities, costs, and historical change.

## Core Belief

The drone is not the product. The drone is the first sensor.

The inspection is not the product. The inspection is structured data acquisition.

The report is not the product. The report is actionable maintenance intelligence.

The website is not the product. The website creates trust.

Every inspection should increase the value of the platform.

## Strategic North Star

Build the highest-quality Property Intelligence platform in the Netherlands.

The path:

1. Premium inspection company.
2. Structured building data from every inspection.
3. Portfolio-level maintenance intelligence.
4. National building intelligence database.
5. AI-assisted defect detection.
6. Predictive maintenance.
7. Digital building twin.
8. Subscription-based Property Intelligence platform.

## Critical Strategic Adjustment

Do not start broad.

RoofSignal should eventually serve many building categories, but the go-to-market motion must start with a narrow wedge:

- Portfolio owners or managers.
- Repeated inspection needs.
- High trust requirement.
- Clear maintenance budget.
- Multiple comparable buildings.
- High value from historical condition tracking.

Best initial wedge:

1. VvE management companies.
2. Small to mid-sized property management companies.
3. Commercial property owners with flat roofs or solar installations.

Reasoning:

- They manage portfolios, not one-off homes.
- They need documentation for boards, owners, budgets, contractors, and MJOP discussions.
- They can become recurring customers.
- A single relationship can unlock many buildings.

Individual homeowners are useful only as proof, not as the main growth engine.

## Business Model

The service business funds the software business.

Every customer funds future automation.

Every inspection improves the dataset.

Every report becomes product research.

Every defect improves the intelligence layer.

Nothing may exist only inside PDFs.

Every observation should become structured information.

## Data Rights Principle

The data moat only exists if RoofSignal has the right to use the data.

From day one, inspection agreements should clearly define:

- Ownership of delivered report.
- RoofSignal's right to retain structured defect data.
- Use of anonymised or aggregated data for product improvement.
- Rules for AI model training.
- Handling of addresses, personal data, license plates, people, and private spaces.
- Retention period.
- Customer opt-outs.

Never assume every photo can become training data.

Separate:

- Customer-owned deliverables.
- RoofSignal-owned structured metadata.
- Anonymised benchmark data.
- AI-training-eligible media.

## Lead Engine

RoofSignal should not search for companies. RoofSignal should search for signals.

Signals include:

- Building age.
- Roof age.
- Flat roofs older than 20 years.
- Storm, hail, or heavy rainfall exposure.
- Visible vegetation or moss.
- Clogged gutters.
- Solar installations.
- Thermography opportunities.
- Municipality maintenance plans.
- Subsidies.
- Permit applications.
- Insurance events.
- Monument status.
- Facade age.
- Industrial roofs.
- Business parks.
- Satellite imagery.
- Street View.
- Public procurement.
- Municipal open data.
- Building registry.
- Public cadastral information where legally available.
- News.
- Google Maps.
- LinkedIn.
- Company websites.

Every signal must support inspection probability, urgency, portfolio value, or outreach relevance.

## Lead Scoring

Lead scores should start as explainable heuristics and evolve with feedback.

Never present scores as objective truth before validation.

Initial dimensions:

- Portfolio fit.
- Building risk.
- Event urgency.
- Visible evidence.
- Budget likelihood.
- Decision-maker accessibility.
- Repeat potential.
- Data value.
- Conversion probability.
- Confidence level.

Example model:

| Dimension | Example signal | Weight |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Portfolio fit | Manager owns or manages many buildings | High |
| Building risk | Flat roof older than 20 years | High |
| Event urgency | Recent storm or heavy rainfall | Medium |
| Visible evidence | Moss, vegetation, clogged gutter | Medium |
| Budget likelihood | VvE/MJOP context or commercial asset | High |
| Data value | Comparable buildings in portfolio | High |
| Conversion probability | Clear contact and decision path | Medium |

Every score should include an explanation.

## Daily Output Standard

The daily lead engine should produce fewer, better opportunities.

Target output:

- Top 20 inspection opportunities.
- Actionable, evidence-based, not generic.

Each lead should include:

- Company.
- Decision maker.
- Address.
- Building type.
- Estimated inspection value.
- Estimated urgency.
- Evidence.
- Why now.
- Supporting observations.
- Personalised outreach suggestion.
- Estimated conversion probability.
- Confidence score.

Quality bar:

If a human cannot understand why this lead is worth contacting in under 30 seconds, the lead is not ready.

## Assumption Challenges

Challenge these assumptions continuously:

1. "Top 20 daily leads" may be too many before quality is proven. Start with top 5 to 10 if evidence quality drops.
2. "Every photo becomes training data" is legally unsafe without explicit data rights.
3. "National database" only matters if the data model is consistent and comparable.
4. "AI defect detection" should not be built before enough labelled, high-quality, diverse observations exist.
5. "Roofing companies as partners" can create conflict of interest. Position clearly as independent inspection intelligence.
6. "Insurance companies as partners" may be high-value but slow-moving. Treat them as phase-later strategic channels.
7. "Digital twin" can become vague. Define it as a condition and maintenance twin, not a generic 3D model.

## Final Objective

Build a company that starts by inspecting buildings and becomes the operating system for building maintenance.

Every decision should move RoofSignal closer to that future.
